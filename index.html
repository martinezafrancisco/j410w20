Economics junior and SLO Smash Community Treasurer James Hasbany said attendance at tournaments has increased over the last few years because of the popularity of the Nintendo Switch, which has the latest edition of the Super Smash Bros. video game, compared to the Nintendo Wii U. "The Wii U was the kind of console that your friend had but nobody else had," Hasbany said. The Nintendo Switch has sold 52.48 million units worldwide as of Dec. 31, 2019, compared to the Wii U's 13.56 million units sold, according to Nintendo financial data. Nozzari fumbled through his backpack to untangle his Nintendo Switch Pro Controller cords. Switch controllers are one of two types of controllers used in the competitive Super Smash Bros. scene, along with old-school Nintendo GameCube controllers. Usage of controllers depends on when a player began playing Smash Bros., according to mechanical engineering senior and club member Jared Palmer. Those who have played longer will often use GameCube controllers, Palmer said, while newer players use the Switch Pro Controller. "I know that it feels very natural and you can really get those nutty little inputs," Nozzari said. "You can get kind of saucy with it." (Feb. 8, 2020 | San Luis Obispo, Calif.)
